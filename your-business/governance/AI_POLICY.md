## Purpose

This policy sets out how [Business Name] uses artificial intelligence tools — what's approved, what requires review, and what's off-limits. It applies to everyone in the organisation who uses AI tools as part of their work.

The goal is not to restrict AI use — it's to make sure we use it responsibly, consistently, and in a way that protects our customers, our data, and our reputation.

- *e.g. Brainstorming ideas, frameworks, and approaches*
- *e.g. Writing and reviewing code (subject to standard code review process)*
- *e.g. Research and information gathering (always verify before treating as fact)*
- *e.g. Creating first drafts of marketing copy (subject to brand review)*

## What AI Must Not Be Used For

The following uses of AI are not permitted:

- *e.g. Accessing or processing personal data covered by GDPR / applicable privacy law without a lawful basis*

## Data Rules — What to Keep Out of AI Tools

> See [DATA_RULES.md](DATA_RULES.md) for the full data classification framework.

**Never paste into an AI tool:**

- Customer personal data (names, emails, addresses, payment information)
- *e.g. Confidential financial data — unreported figures, forecasts, M&A*
- *e.g. Proprietary source code marked as confidential*
- *e.g. Staff personal information*
- *e.g. Data subject to NDA or client confidentiality agreements*
- *e.g. Health or medical information*

**Safe to use:**

- Anonymised or aggregated data
- Publicly available information
- Internal documents not marked confidential
- *[Any other categories you explicitly approve]*

**When in doubt:** Remove or anonymise before pasting. If you're not sure, ask *[designated contact]* before proceeding.
