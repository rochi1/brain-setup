# Task Library

> **What this file is:** A library of prebuilt prompts for the business tasks your team does repeatedly. Each is designed to work on top of your Master Prompt — so your context, voice, and constraints are already loaded before you run any of these.
>
> **How to use:** Load your Master Prompt first, then copy and paste whichever task prompt you need. Fill in the `[bracketed placeholders]` with your specifics.
>
> **Add your own:** The last section shows the format. Any time you write a prompt that works well for a recurring task, add it here.

---

## Content & Copy

### Blog Post

```
Write a blog post on the following topic: [TOPIC]

Target audience: [Which persona from AUDIENCE.md, or describe]
Goal of this post: [Inform / build trust / generate leads / SEO / other]
Desired length: [e.g. 600 words / 1,200 words / long-form]
Tone notes: [Any deviation from our default voice for this piece]
Key points to cover: [List 3–5 or leave blank to let AI structure it]
Call to action: [What should the reader do at the end?]

Use our brand voice. Avoid generic AI article structure (no "In conclusion..." or "In today's world..."). Lead with what's interesting, not with context-setting.
```

### Email — Marketing / Campaign

```
Write a marketing email for the following campaign: [CAMPAIGN NAME / PURPOSE]

Audience: [Segment — new leads / existing customers / lapsed customers / other]
Goal: [What action do we want them to take?]
Key message: [The single most important thing this email communicates]
Offer or hook: [What we're offering, announcing, or sharing]
CTA: [The specific action — link text, button copy, or reply instruction]
Tone: [Any adjustment from our default voice]

Keep the subject line, preview text, and body distinct. Write 2 subject line options. No hollow openers ("Hope this finds you well"). Get to the point fast.
```

---

### Email — Transactional / Operational

```
Write a transactional email for the following trigger: [TRIGGER — e.g. welcome, purchase confirmation, password reset, trial ending]

What the reader has just done or what has just happened: [Context]
What they need to know: [Key information]
What they need to do next (if anything): [Action]
Tone: [Functional and clear — not a marketing moment unless specified]

Keep it short. Every sentence should be there for a reason.
```

### Social Media Post

```
Write a social media post for the following: [TOPIC / CONTENT PIECE / ANNOUNCEMENT]

Platform: [LinkedIn / X (Twitter) / Instagram / Facebook / other]
Goal: [Awareness / engagement / clicks / shares]
Hook approach: [Question / bold claim / statistic / story / other — or let AI decide]
Link or CTA: [If applicable]
Length: [Short / medium / thread — or platform default]

Adapt tone for the platform. No hashtag stuffing. No "Drop a comment below!" unless it's genuinely relevant.
```

### Website Copy — Page Section

```
Write copy for the following section of our website: [PAGE NAME + SECTION — e.g. Homepage hero, About page intro, Pricing page header]

What this section needs to do: [e.g. Immediately communicate our value, build trust, explain the product]
Reader's state of mind when they arrive here: [e.g. Sceptical and comparing options / Warm lead already interested / First-time visitor who doesn't know us]
Key message: [The one thing they must leave this section knowing or feeling]
Word count target: [e.g. Headline + 2 sentences / Under 100 words / etc.]

Follow our brand voice exactly. Prioritise clarity over cleverness.
```

### Case Study

```
Help me write a case study for the following customer story: [CUSTOMER NAME / PSEUDONYM]

Customer profile: [Who they are — industry, size, role of main contact]
The situation before: [What problem or situation brought them to us]
What we did: [The solution, product, or service used]
The result: [Outcomes — ideally with specific numbers or before/after comparisons]
A quote from the customer (if available): [Paste here, or note if not available]
Target audience for this case study: [Which persona it's meant to persuade]

Structure: Situation → Challenge → Solution → Result → Quote. Keep it factual. Let the numbers do the work. Write in third person.
```

## Research & Analysis

### Market Research Summary

```
Summarise the key insights from the following research material and tell me what it means for our business: [PASTE RESEARCH / REPORT / DATA]

Focus on:
- What's most relevant to our audience and market position
- Anything that challenges our current assumptions
- Opportunities we're not currently addressing
- Risks we should be aware of

Format: Key findings (bullet points) → So what for our business (brief narrative) → Recommended actions (prioritised list).
```

---

### Competitor Analysis

```
Analyse the following competitor: [COMPETITOR NAME]

Sources to review (paste or attach): [Website copy / pricing page / reviews / LinkedIn / other]

I want to understand:
1. How they position themselves and who they're targeting
2. What they do well that we should be aware of
3. Where they're weak or where customers are frustrated
4. How we should think about them in our own positioning

Be honest — if they do something better than us, say so. I need an accurate picture, not a flattering one.
```

---

### Customer Feedback Analysis

```
Analyse the following customer feedback and summarise the key themes: [PASTE FEEDBACK — reviews, survey responses, support tickets, interview notes]

Tell me:
1. The top 3–5 things customers value most
2. The top 3–5 pain points or frustrations
3. Any patterns in how customers describe the problem we solve (their language, not ours)
4. Anything surprising or that challenges what we think we know

Format: Themes → Direct quotes that illustrate each theme → Recommendations.
```

## Internal & Operations

### Meeting Agenda

```
Create an agenda for the following meeting: [MEETING NAME / PURPOSE]

Duration: [e.g. 30 min / 60 min / 90 min]
Attendees: [Roles, not names]
Goal of the meeting: [What does a successful meeting produce?]
Topics to cover: [List or leave blank to let AI suggest based on meeting purpose]
Pre-read or preparation required: [If any]

Format each agenda item with: topic, owner, time allocation, and desired output (decision / update / discussion).
```

---

### Meeting Notes → Action Items

```
Turn the following meeting notes into a clean summary with action items: [PASTE NOTES]

Produce:
1. One-paragraph summary of what was discussed and decided
2. Action items in a table: Action | Owner | Due date
3. Open questions or decisions deferred

Be concise. Do not include everything discussed — only what matters going forward.
```

### Job Description

```
Write a job description for the following role: [JOB TITLE]

Key responsibilities: [List 4–6 or describe in plain English]
Must-have requirements: [Skills, experience, qualifications that are non-negotiable]
Nice-to-have: [Preferred but not required]
Team / reporting structure: [Who they work with and report to]
Anything important about our culture or way of working: [What someone needs to thrive here]

Write in our voice. Be specific and honest — describe the job as it actually is. Avoid inflated corporate job description language. Be clear about what makes this role challenging, not just appealing.
```

### SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)

```
Write a standard operating procedure for the following process: [PROCESS NAME]

Purpose of this process: [What it achieves and why it matters]
Who follows this process: [Role(s)]
Trigger: [What starts this process]
Steps: [List what you know — AI will structure and fill gaps]
Tools or systems involved: [Any software, templates, or platforms]
Common mistakes or failure points: [If known]
How do we know it's done correctly: [Success criteria or quality check]

Format: Purpose → Scope → Trigger → Step-by-step (numbered, with owner per step) → Quality check → Exception handling.
```

## Strategy

### SWOT Analysis

```
Run a SWOT analysis for the following: [OUR BUSINESS / A SPECIFIC PRODUCT / A DECISION / A MARKET ENTRY]

Use the context you have about our business. For each quadrant, give 4–6 specific points — not generic ones.

After the SWOT, give me a "So what?" — the 2–3 most important strategic implications based on the analysis.

Be direct. If there are genuine weaknesses or threats, name them clearly.
```

---

### Decision Framework

```
Help me think through the following decision: [DESCRIBE THE DECISION]

Context: [What's driving this? What do we know? What are the constraints?]
Options being considered: [List them, or ask AI to generate options]
What matters most in making this decision: [Criteria — e.g. speed, cost, risk, reversibility]

Structure your response as:
1. Restate the decision clearly
2. Evaluate each option against the criteria
3. Your recommendation and why
4. Key risks or unknowns that could change the answer
5. What we'd need to believe for each option to be right
```

## Adding Your Own Tasks

Use this format to add prompts for recurring tasks in your business:

```
### [Task Name]

[One sentence on when to use this prompt]

[The prompt — with [BRACKETED PLACEHOLDERS] for the parts that change each time]

[Any formatting or output instructions specific to this task]
```

Good candidates for custom tasks: anything your team prompts AI to do more than twice a week with similar structure each time.
