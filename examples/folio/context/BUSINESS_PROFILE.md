# Business Profile — Folio

---

## Overview

**Business name:** Folio

**Industry / sector:** B2B SaaS — Creative Agency Tools

**One-line description:** Folio is a client portal and project hub that helps creative agencies deliver work, collect feedback, and get approvals without the email chaos.

**Founded:** 2022, remotely distributed (HQ: Austin, TX)

**Current stage:** Growth stage — 1,200 paying customers, Series A raised, scaling sales and marketing.

---

## What We Do

### The problem we solve
Creative agencies — branding studios, web design shops, video production companies — spend an embarrassing amount of their time on client admin that has nothing to do with the actual work. A typical project involves files scattered across Dropbox and Google Drive, feedback buried in email threads, approval sign-offs on WhatsApp, and version confusion that nobody admits until something goes live wrong. Project managers spend 30–40% of their week chasing clients, resending files, and reconstructing who said what and when. Clients are frustrated because they don't know where to look. Agencies are frustrated because they built a creative business, not a file management operation.

### Our solution
Folio gives every client project a dedicated portal — a single, branded space where deliverables are shared, feedback is collected in context, approvals are logged, and conversations are tied to the work they're about. Agencies invite clients to their portal, share work in stages, and get sign-off without a single email attachment. Everything is timestamped, version-tracked, and searchable. The agency looks professional. The client feels taken care of. The work moves forward.

### How we make money
SaaS subscription — three tiers (Starter, Growth, Team) billed monthly or annually. Annual billing at ~20% discount. Primary revenue driver is Growth and Team tiers. Starter is the acquisition vehicle.

## Mission & Values

### Mission statement
To give creative agencies their time back — so the people they hired to do great work can actually do it.

### Core values

- **Built for the work, not for the demo:** Every feature has to solve a real problem that real agencies have. If it looks good in a sales demo but nobody uses it, we cut it.
- **Honest and direct:** We don't oversell, we don't hide bad news, and we don't write marketing copy that makes us sound like something we're not.
- **Simple by default, powerful when needed:** Most of our customers don't want to configure anything. The defaults should work. Power users can go deeper.
- **The client experience matters as much as the agency experience:** We're a B2B product but our users' clients interact with us too. If the client portal is confusing, our customers look bad. That's on us.
- **Opinionated where it helps:** We make decisions so our customers don't have to. When there's one obviously right way to do something, we build that and don't offer seventeen alternatives.

## Who We Serve

### Primary customer
Independent creative agencies with 5–30 staff. Typically: branding studios, web design agencies, digital marketing agencies, and video production companies. The buyer is usually the agency owner, studio director, or head of operations. They've already tried cobbling together Notion, Dropbox, and email and they know there has to be a better way. They're not looking for a project management tool — they have one. They're looking for a client-facing layer that makes them look like they have their act together.

### Secondary customers
Freelancers running a small but growing practice (1–4 people) who are moving upmarket and need to look more professional. Also mid-size agencies (30–100 staff) where Folio sits alongside a more complex internal PM tool and handles purely the client-facing layer.

### Who we are NOT for
- Enterprises with procurement processes, SSO requirements, and legal review — we're not enterprise-ready and we don't pretend to be yet
- Internal project management — we're a client-facing tool, not a replacement for Asana or Linear
- Businesses with very low client touchpoints (e.g. pure e-commerce, physical product companies with no ongoing client relationships)
- Agencies who want to heavily customise every pixel of the experience — we're opinionated and not infinitely configurable

## Market Position

### Key competitors
Notion (repurposed), Basecamp, ClientVenue, Copilot (formerly Portal), Dubsado

### How we're different
We're the only tool in this space built specifically for the deliverable review and approval workflow — not adapted from a general-purpose PM or CRM tool. Our inline feedback on images, video, and PDFs is significantly better than anything in our category. We also have the best client-side experience: clients don't need to create an account or download anything to interact with their portal. That matters because the agency's client didn't choose to use Folio — the agency did. Making it frictionless for them is where most competitors fail.

### Positioning statement
"For creative agencies who are tired of managing client work across email and Google Drive, Folio is the client portal that makes delivering and approving work feel effortless — unlike general-purpose tools like Notion or Basecamp, which were built for internal teams and show it."

## History & Context

### Key milestones
- **2022 Q1:** Founded by two ex-agency operators (combined 18 years running branding and web agencies) who built Folio to solve their own problem
- **2022 Q4:** Launched publicly, 50 paying customers within 60 days
- **2023 Q2:** Hit $1M ARR — faster than projected, primarily through word of mouth in agency communities
- **2024 Q1:** Raised $4.2M Series A. Hired first sales team.
- **2024 Q3:** Launched Team tier with white-label option — opened up reseller/agency-of-record use cases
- **2025 Q2:** Hit $6M ARR, 1,200 customers

### Current focus
Converting inbound interest into paid customers faster (sales cycle is longer than expected for SMB), reducing churn at the Starter tier (often people who weren't the right fit to begin with), and building the integrations ecosystem (HubSpot, Zapier, Slack) to reduce switching costs.

### Known challenges
- Starter-tier churn is higher than we'd like — some customers sign up before they're ready for a client portal
- We're often compared to tools that do far more (ClickUp, Monday) by prospects who conflate "project management" with what we do — messaging needs to be clearer about what we're not
- White-label is our fastest growing feature request but also our most complex to build well — moving carefully
